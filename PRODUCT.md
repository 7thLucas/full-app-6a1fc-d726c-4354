# Glimwood - Product Brief

## Product Overview
Glimwood is a web-based pixel-art platformer game playable directly in the browser. It delivers ONE polished first level that serves as both a playable demo and a visual poster for the indie game vibe.

## Target Audience
- **Primary**: Indie/Steam crowd who appreciate polished 2D platformers
- **Secondary**: Nostalgic 30-somethings who grew up with Mario, Sonic, and modern hits like Celeste
- **Tertiary**: Casual web gamers looking for a quick, satisfying play session

## Users
- **The Nostalgic Gamer**: Wants pixel-perfect aesthetics and tight controls reminiscent of SNES-era platformers
- **The Indie Enthusiast**: Looks for unique art direction and atmospheric world-building
- **The Speedrunner-Curious**: Enjoys discovering hidden paths and mastering jump physics

## Brand Identity
- **Name**: Glimwood (suggests a magical, glowing forest)
- **Vibe**: Playful, energetic, colorful, nostalgic — polished enough to belong on Steam
- **Tone**: Whimsical but not childish; mysterious yet inviting; adventurous

## Strategic Principles
1. **Feel over features**: Tight, responsive jump/run physics trumps content quantity
2. **Visual polish as proof**: The single level must look and feel finished, not prototype-y
3. **Hidden depth**: Secret areas reward exploration and create replay value
4. **Audio that sings**: Chiptune music and crisp SFX make every action satisfying
5. **Browser-first**: No installs, no friction — playable instantly

## Core Gameplay Features
- **Hero Character**: Full animation set (idle, run, jump, landing, celebrate)
- **Movement**: Tight, responsive controls with no floatiness or sticky edges
- **Enemies**: At least 1 enemy type with distinct behavior
- **Collectibles**: Coins/gems with satisfying pickup feedback (visual + audio)
- **Secrets**: 1+ hidden path or secret area
- **Checkpoints**: Fair-but-engaging difficulty curve
- **Audio**: Chiptune background music + crisp SFX for jumps/hits/pickups
- **Visuals**: Parallax background layers, animated environmental detail (clouds, leaves, sparkles)

## Anti-References
- NOT a mobile-style tap game
- NOT a roguelike or procedurally generated
- NOT a heavy AAA experience
- NOT educational or gamified learning
- NOT a clone of any specific existing game

## Success Criteria
- Player can complete the level within 3-5 minutes
- Controls feel "right" within the first 30 seconds
- At least one "wow" moment (secret reveal, satisfying animation, audio peak)
- Visually shareable as a screenshot/poster


#CORE TRUTH:
# Glimwood — Core Truth

## Name
**Glimwood** — a single-word indie-Steam-ready title evoking the glow of crystal caves and the warmth of mushroom forests. Reads like Celeste, Hollow Knight, Shovel Knight — single, ownable, memorable.

## What it is
A high-quality pixel-art platformer inspired by classic 8/16-bit games (Mario lineage) with a modern indie soul. Cheerful cartoony hero, expressive animation set (run, jump, crouch, climb, celebrate), pixel-perfect sprite sheets for smooth movement. Vibrant whimsical worlds: lush forests, bouncy mushroom hills, sparkling waterfalls, glowing crystal caves, floating sky islands. Layered parallax, dynamic lighting, environmental life (falling leaves, water ripples, drifting clouds, mist). Catchy chiptune-inspired soundtrack and crunchy SFX for jumps, hits, pickups.

## The problem it solves
Modern platformers fall into three traps:
1. **Too floaty** — jumps feel imprecise, players overshoot, skill stops mattering.
2. **Too punishing** — one-hit deaths, scarce checkpoints, exploration feels scary.
3. **Too forgettable** — generic art, recycled enemies, no memorable hooks.

Glimwood fixes the floaty + punishing + forgettable triad by combining responsive Mario-style physics, fair-but-engaging challenge with moderately generous checkpoints, and a vibrant memorable world full of personality.

## Target audience
**Primary (hybrid):** Indie/Steam crowd (Celeste / Shovel Knight fans) + nostalgic 30-somethings (SNES/Mario veterans). They want tight, predictable physics with charming retro polish, layered difficulty (some obvious paths, some hidden), moderately generous checkpoints that reward mastery without punishing exploration, and secrets/easter eggs that make the world worth obsessing over.

**Secondary:** Younger players discovering the genre (bright colors, generous feedback, low friction) and speedrunners (transparent frame-consistent physics, optional advanced routing).

**Design wedge:** *Approachable depth* — easy to start, rich to master.

## Day-one MVP (the one thing to nail first)
**One polished first level** that doubles as a playable demo AND a visual poster. Must deliver:
1. **Tight responsive controls** — run/jump/landing arcs perfectly tuned, no floatiness or sticky edges, players feel in control from frame one.
2. **Crisp pixel-art polish** — tiles, platforms, enemies, collectibles, parallax layers, environmental details that pop while staying readable.
3. **Natural mechanic introduction** — running, jumping, basic enemy interactions, breakable blocks, collectibles — taught through level design, not tutorial overlays.
4. **Hero personality** — idle/jump/landing/celebrate animations, plus environmental life (falling leaves, drifting clouds, sparkly coins) that make the world feel alive.
5. **Vibe-setting** — playful, energetic, nostalgic, visually distinctive enough that within 30–60 seconds players know "this is Glimwood."

This single slice sells the entire pitch and bakes in the foundational polish every subsequent level inherits.

## Mechanics & content scope (post-MVP layering)
- Breakable blocks, collectible coins/gems, hidden power-ups
- Moving platforms, springboards, ropes/ladders
- Enemies with distinct readable designs (walking slimes, hopping creatures, flying critters)
- Multi-layer parallax, dynamic lighting, ambient effects
- Per-world coherent color palettes (modernized retro)
- Animated background objects, foreground obstacles, hidden easter eggs
- Chiptune soundtrack with per-world variation, satisfying SFX library

## Vibe
Playful, energetic, colorful, nostalgic — polished enough for modern indie Steam appeal. Approachable on the surface, deep underneath. Every screen should feel like a place worth lingering in.
